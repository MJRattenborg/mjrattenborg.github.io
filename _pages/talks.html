**About the Workshop**
The workshop aims to encourage new research at the intersection of behavioral and labor economics. Its goal is to provide a platform for PhD students and recent graduates to present their research and receive feedback on their work. Contributions from all areas and applications of behavioral and labor economics are welcome, including empirical and theoretical research. The workshop targets current PhD students and early-career researchers who have graduated in 2025 or later and are working as postdocs and assistant professors, or equivalent.
Costs
The conference will be hosted and sponsored by CEBI. There is no conference fee. Conference meals will be covered for all invited participants. Participants are responsible for their own travel and accommodation expenses.
